DIY Wind tunnel

A cheap, quick, and efficient wind tunnel for educational purposes.

  • Get started!

Gather the materials

You'll be needing a few items: 1x electric motor + propeller + esc, 1x battery for said motor, 1x arduino microcontroller, 1x servo, 1x breadboard, 2x potentiometer, LOTS of straws (800+), some cardboard, and a handful of jumper wires.

Prepare the tunnel

Stuff the box with straws. Make sure they can't just fall out. Attach the motor + prop to the front, with the propeller facing outward and wind heading into the tunnel. Create a small flap with some carboard and attach that to the servo and place that a few inches behind the straws. Tape the boxes up securely and mount the remaining hardware on the side as pictures.

Program the arduino

Hook up the potentiometers to the analog inputs and the servo and motor to pwm digital pins. Verify polarity on the cables. Map the input value of the potentiometers to corresponding values for the motor and servo. Write the code, compile, and upload to the arduino. Now enjoy your windtunnel!
